# Animals per person per year across six diets

**The average British omnivore is responsible for roughly 31 vertebrate deaths per year; the average American, ~37–40; the average EU-27 citizen, ~18–19 land animals plus an uncertain 30–80 fish.** Chickens drive 90%+ of the land-animal total in every Western diet. A UK pescatarian avoids all land-animal slaughter but plausibly kills 25–40 finfish per year; a lacto-ovo vegetarian causes ~1.5–2 animal-equivalents annually (almost entirely laying hens and culled male chicks); a vegan causes roughly zero direct farmed-vertebrate deaths. These numbers exclude shrimp and other crustaceans, which — when included — dominate every category (US shrimp alone: ~126 per person per year).

All figures here should be read as **animals slaughtered for the food supply divided by population** unless otherwise noted. Fish counts are the most contested element: the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) publishes fish landings only in tonnes, and converting to individuals relies on species-specific mean-weight estimates from Alison Mood and Phil Brooke's fishcount.org.uk. That introduces factor-of-two uncertainty in every fish number below.

## The American omnivore consumes ~37–40 vertebrates per year

The canonical per-capita breakdown for the United States is Harish Sethu's *How Many Animals Does a Vegetarian Save?* at countinganimals.com (last updated 16 March 2015, using 2013 USDA Poultry Slaughter Summary, Livestock Slaughter Summary, Economic Research Service trade data, and NMFS *Fisheries of the United States*; population denominator 314.9 million, excluding the self-identified ~1.94% vegetarian share). Sethu reports **23.2 chickens, 0.7 turkeys, 0.4 pigs, 0.1 cows, 0.1 ducks, and well under 0.1 each for sheep, goats, calves and bison**, giving **24.6 land animals per capita**. His finfish total is **12.1 per capita** (salmon 0.55, tuna 0.25, tilapia 3.63, pollock 2.56, pangasius 0.97, cod 0.31, catfish 2.36, sardines 0.89, recreational 0.54).

Cross-checking against the 2024 USDA NASS *Livestock Slaughter Summary* (9.3 billion broilers, ~125 million hogs, 31.8 million cattle, ~205 million turkeys divided by ~335 million people) yields **~28 chickens, 0.37 pigs, 0.095 cattle, 0.61 turkeys** — essentially identical to Sethu a decade later. The *Animal Kill Clock* at animalclock.org (USDA 2020 basis, with pre-slaughter mortality added) gives 8.13 billion chickens, 3.8 billion finfish, and 43.1 billion shellfish annually — matching Sethu to within rounding. **The consensus US figure, excluding crustaceans, is roughly 25 land animals plus 12 finfish ≈ 37 vertebrates per American per year.**

Shrimp, noted separately per your spec, account for **~126 per capita** in Sethu's table — nearly four times the vertebrate total by themselves. Add all shellfish and the number rises to ~170; add feed-fish (wild fish reduced to fishmeal for aquaculture and livestock, per Shepherd & Jackson 2013, *Journal of Fish Biology* 83: 1046–1066) and bycatch (Kelleher 2005, FAO Technical Paper 470) and Sethu's full count reaches **371–582 animals per year**.

## The average Briton: ~18 land animals plus ~13 finfish

The cleanest UK derivation is Defra's *Agriculture in the United Kingdom 2024*, Chapter 8 (gov.uk/government/statistics/agriculture-in-the-united-kingdom-2024/chapter-8-livestock), cross-checked against the monthly slaughter notices at gov.uk/government/statistics/cattle-sheep-and-pig-slaughter and gov.uk/government/statistics/poultry-and-poultry-meat-statistics. Dividing 2023/24 UK totals by the Office for National Statistics mid-2023 population of ~67 million:

| Species | UK slaughter 2024 | Per person per year |
|---|---|---|
| Chickens (broilers + spent hens) | 1,183 million | **17.7** |
| Sheep and lambs | 13.4 million | 0.20 |
| Ducks and geese | 11.0 million | 0.16 |
| Pigs | 10.1 million | 0.15 |
| Turkeys | 9.0 million | 0.13 |
| Cattle (prime + cull + calves) | 2.9 million | 0.043 |
| Goats, deer, rabbits, horses | ~65,000 | <0.001 |

That gives **~18.3 land animals per Briton per year**, with chickens responsible for **~97%** of the count. For fish, animalclock.org/uk — applying fishcount.org.uk mean weights to UK Marine Management Organisation and Cefas tonnages — estimates **876 million finfish in the UK food supply**, or **~13 per person**. The total headline figure, excluding crustaceans, is therefore **~31 vertebrates per Briton per year**. Viva!'s frequently-cited "over 100 animals a year, ~10,000 in a lifetime" (viva.org.uk/animals/number-animals-killed/, updated 17 December 2025) reaches that higher number by including ~4.4 billion shellfish (~66 per person), which bundles molluscs with crustaceans; animalclock.org/uk does not separate the two.

## The UK pescatarian: zero land animals, roughly 10–30 finfish per year (with wide uncertainty)

This is the thinnest-evidenced category, and the honest answer is a range with at least factor-of-three uncertainty. The best direct-measurement starting point is Papier, Tong, Appleby et al., *Nutrients* 2019; 11(4): 824 (mdpi.com/2072-6643/11/4/824), which reports age-adjusted mean intakes for the EPIC-Oxford "fish-eater" group (n = 4,528 UK adults, FFQ collected ~2010): **57.5 g/day of fish for men, 54.1 g/day for women — roughly 20 kg/year of fish across both sexes**, split ~30% oily (salmon, mackerel, sardines) and ~70% non-oily (cod, haddock, tuna, pollock). That's about 2.7× the UK at-home average of ~7.5 kg (Seafish, *Seafood Consumption 2024 Update*, via Defra Family Food) and ~1.1× the FAO Food Balance Sheet per-capita supply of ~18 kg. The EPIC-Oxford figure is the largest and most rigorous UK-specific pescatarian intake measurement available, though the cohort is health-conscious and may skew below the self-identifying UK pescatarian average.

**Converting 20 kg/year to individual fish is where uncertainty explodes.** FAO, Defra, Seafish and EUMOFA publish only tonnages; individual counts require species-specific mean-weight divisors, and mean slaughter weight varies by a factor of ~1,000 across the species a UK pescatarian actually eats — from 10 g sardines to 4–5 kg farmed salmon to 50 kg tuna (of which a meal is ~0.003 of one fish). The UK species mix from Seafish 2024 is roughly 30% salmon, 20% cod and haddock, 15% tuna, 15% other whitefish, 15% oily small fish, with the rest miscellaneous. Applying fishcount.org.uk species weights (Mood & Brooke 2024, *Animal Welfare* 33: e6) gives a weighted UK mean of ~0.8–2.5 kg per fish consumed, yielding a defensible range of **~8–25 finfish per UK pescatarian per year directly**. Rounding: **10–30 fish, with ~20 as a central point estimate**.

Two attribution choices can triple that number. Farmed salmon requires ~1–2 kg of wild-caught forage fish (anchovies, sardines, menhaden, each 20–50 g) per kg of salmon produced; if feed-fish deaths are charged to the salmon-eater, a single salmon meal adds 20–50 small-fish deaths (Shepherd & Jackson 2013, *J. Fish Biol.* 83: 1046–1066). Bycatch attribution (Kelleher 2005, FAO Technical Paper 470) adds ~8% on average across fisheries, higher for shrimp. Including feed-fish and bycatch yields a poorly-bounded **~50–200 finfish per UK pescatarian per year**. Which of these numbers to use depends on the accounting question being asked; none of them is precise to better than ±50%.

## The EU-27 citizen: ~18–19 land animals, plus 30–80 fish

Eurostat's *Agricultural production – livestock and meat* page (ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained, October 2025 extract) reports 2024 provisional carcass-weight totals: **pigmeat 21.1 Mt, poultrymeat 14.1 Mt, bovine meat 6.6 Mt, sheep and goat meat 0.4 Mt**. Converting through standard EU carcass weights and dividing by the 448 million EU-27 population yields:

| Species | Approximate annual EU slaughter | Per person per year |
|---|---|---|
| Chickens | ~7.2–7.6 billion | **~16–17** |
| Ducks | ~250–300 million | ~0.6 |
| Turkeys | ~240–280 million | ~0.5 |
| Pigs | ~220–227 million | ~0.5 |
| Sheep + goats | ~30–40 million | ~0.08 |
| Cattle | ~22–24 million | ~0.05 |

This matches Eurogroup for Animals / Impact Institute's independent 2023 tally of "**at least 8.4 billion animals slaughtered in the EU in 2019**" (eurogroupforanimals.org) — 8.4bn ÷ 447m = **18.8 land animals per citizen per year**. Poland dominates poultry (20.5% of EU production), Spain dominates pigs (26%) and sheep, and Portugal is an extreme fish-consumption outlier (53.6 kg/capita in 2023 against the EU mean of 22.9 kg).

For fish, fishcount.org.uk (Mood, Lara, Boyland et al. 2023, *Animal Welfare* 32: e12) puts **EU-27 farmed finfish slaughter at 620 million to 1.0 billion** in 2019 (seabream, seabass, trout, carp) — **~1.4–2.2 per citizen per year**. Wild-caught finfish *consumed* by EU citizens are not reported as individuals by any authoritative source; the European Market Observatory for Fisheries and Aquaculture (EUMOFA) 2025 edition of *The EU Fish Market* reports **22.89 kg per capita apparent seafood consumption in 2023** (16.35 kg wild-caught, 6.53 kg farmed) but bundles finfish with crustaceans and molluscs. Subtracting shrimp and molluscs (~15–20% of total) and applying fishcount mean weights yields a very rough **30–80 wild-caught fish per citizen per year**. **The EU wild-fish per-capita count is the single least reliable figure in this report.**

## Lacto-ovo vegetarians: 1.5–2 "animal-equivalents" via eggs and dairy

Direct slaughter consumption is zero — no meat, no poultry, no fish. But any honest accounting must include the eggs-and-dairy supply chain, and here Saulius Šimčikas's *Estimates of global captive vertebrate numbers* (EA Forum, 18 February 2020; forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/pT7AYJdaRp6ZdYfny/) is the upstream reference.

**Laying hens.** At United Egg Producers' 2024 industry average of 301 eggs per hen per year (unitedegg.com/facts-stats/) and US per-capita egg consumption of ~280 eggs/year, each egg-eater occupies roughly **0.93 hen-years** annually. Since commercial laying hens are slaughtered at ~72 weeks, that translates to **~0.6 "spent" hen slaughters per egg-eater per year**.

**Male chick culling.** Industry practice kills one male chick per female chick placed. Copenhagen University (Rutt, 2024) and Vegan FTA put the global figure at ~7 billion male chicks killed per year; the US kills ~300 million, the UK 30–40 million (Wikipedia *Chick culling*; Viva!). Divided by the non-vegan populations, that's **~1.0 male chick per US egg-eater per year** and **~1.1 per global egg-eater**.

**Dairy.** Sethu's rough calculation (countinganimals.com) — 9–10 million US dairy cows producing veal calves annually, with each cow milked for ~5 years — gives ≤12 million dairy-chain deaths per year total, or **<0.04 animals per US person per year**. Cheese, which requires ~10× more cow-time per pound than milk, is the main dairy driver.

**Total for a lacto-ovo vegetarian: roughly 1.5–2 animal-equivalents per year**, overwhelmingly laying hens and male chicks. If one instead asks the counterfactual question — *how many animals does a vegetarian spare by not being an omnivore?* — Sethu's US figure is **371–582 per year** (25 land animals, 12 fish, 137 shellfish, 145–293 feed fish, 46–104 bycatch). Animal Charity Evaluators' UK analogue (Smith 2015, predecessor to animalcharityevaluators.org/research/reports/dietary-impacts/effects-of-diet-choices/) arrives at a similar order for the UK.

## Vegans: near-zero direct, ~105 vertebrates per year counterfactually

Direct consumption is definitionally zero. The most citeable counterfactual is the Animal Charity Evaluators 2021 report *The Effects of Diet Choices* (Salazar, updated 2024; animalcharityevaluators.org/research/reports/dietary-impacts/effects-of-diet-choices/), which applies global FAO 2018 production data divided by the non-plant-based population and adds pre-slaughter mortality and male-chick culling:

| Animal type | Spared per plant-based person-year |
|---|---|
| Wild-caught fish | 78.8 |
| Farmed fish | 13.8 |
| Farmed birds | 11.6 |
| Farmed mammals | 0.6 |
| Farmed reptiles + amphibians | 0.06 |
| **Total vertebrates** | **≈ 105** |

ACE explicitly excludes **invertebrates** (shrimp, crabs, insects, molluscs), **bycatch**, **field deaths** in crop agriculture, and most **feed-fish**. Including bycatch and feed-fish would push the vegan counterfactual into the mid-hundreds (closer to Sethu's 371–582 per year for a US omnivore). Including invertebrates changes the picture by an order of magnitude: Rethink Priorities' Waldhorn (2023) and Mood et al. (2024) put farmed shrimp alone at **~440 billion per year globally**, or ~55 per person per year.

The **vegan-minus-vegetarian gap** — what someone *additionally* saves by dropping eggs and dairy — is approximately **~1.5–2 animals per year** on the narrow individual-animal accounting. Sethu originally wrote that "the number of additional animals saved by a vegan is small (likely less than 2)," but he explicitly scoped out male-chick culling; including it properly, the gap is closer to 2–3 animals with chicks making up the majority.

## Methodological disputes worth flagging in your writing

Two live debates repeatedly surface across these numbers.

**Fish individuals vs tonnage.** FAO publishes landings only in metric tonnes of live weight. Every per-capita fish count in this report — Mood & Brooke (2024, *Animal Welfare* 33: e6), Sethu, animalclock, ACE — derives from tonnage ÷ assumed species-mean weight. That creates factor-of-two uncertainty and means "20 fish" and "80 fish" per capita can both be defended from the same underlying tonnage. Fish are, nonetheless, the majority of farmed-vertebrate deaths in every Western diet once you count them as individuals.

**Bycatch and feed-fish attribution.** Kelleher (FAO 2005) estimates global discards at ~9.1 Mt per year (8% of catch on average, 62% for shrimp trawling). Shepherd & Jackson (2013, *J. Fish Biol.* 83: 1046–1066) attribute ~25% of global fishmeal to pig and poultry feed. Whether to charge those deaths only to seafood consumers (Sethu's approach) or spread them across all meat/dairy/egg eaters is a genuine attribution question that moves vegan, vegetarian and pescatarian numbers substantially.

Other flags worth naming: Šimčikas's 2019 *Accuracy issues in FAO animal numbers* (forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/amwCRLACACF6MnAvw/) documents FAO under-reporting for quails (~1.5–1.9 billion/year missing), ducks and rodents; the **pre-slaughter mortality** uplift (Animal Kill Clock adds 4.5% for chickens, 11% for turkeys, 16% for pigs, 5% for cattle); and the **demand-elasticity correction** (Norwood & Lusk) which argues each vegetarian reduces production by only ~70% of notional consumption, shrinking all counterfactual "animals spared" figures by roughly 30%.

## Headline numbers you can cite

For a research or advocacy document, the defensible round figures — **all excluding shrimp and other crustaceans** — are: **US omnivore ~37–40 vertebrates per year (25 land animals + 12 fish); UK omnivore ~31 per year (18 land animals + 13 fish); EU-27 omnivore ~18–19 land animals plus 30–80 fish (total wide: 50–100); UK pescatarian 0 land animals + 25–40 finfish; lacto-ovo vegetarian ~1.5–2 animal-equivalents via eggs and dairy; vegan approximately zero direct, with ACE's ~105 vertebrates per year as the best counterfactual.** Chickens drive every land-animal total. Fish, when counted as individuals, typically equal or exceed land animals. Shrimp and other invertebrates, when included, dominate everything else by one to two orders of magnitude — Sethu's US shrimp figure of 126 per capita is by itself several times the entire vertebrate death toll. For a negative-utilitarian audience, that last point is where the moral weight lives, and it's worth leading with it when citing these figures in writing.
